Mitosis

There are several sub-stages/phases of Mitosis.


  • 2nd stage of the cell cycle
  • has 4 sub-stages/phases
  • Results in 2 nuclei
  • DNA distribution between 2 daughter cells



  • Prophase

  • First sub-stage/phase of Mitosis

  • Chromosomes condense
    Centrioles move to opposite ends of the cell
  • Spindle fibers form between the ends of the cell
    The nuclear envelope dissolves


  • Metaphase

  • 2nd sub-stage/phase of Mitosis.
  • Chromosomes attach to spindle fibers
  • Chromosomes align in the center of the cell



  • Anaphase
  • 3rd sub-stage/phase of Mitosis.

  • Centromere splits
  • The chromatids split, and are now called chromosomes
  • Pulled to opposite ends of the now stretched cell


  • Telophase
  • 4th and last sub-stage/phase of Mitosis
  • Nuclear envelopes form
  •                          Spindle Fibers disperse
